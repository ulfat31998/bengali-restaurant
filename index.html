Our Story

A Family Recipe Passed Through Generations

Sonar Bangla began in a small Kolkata kitchen, where our grandmother, Thakuma, simmered mustard-fish curries and slow-cooked mutton over open flame for the whole neighbourhood. Her belief was simple — food is love made edible, and every guest is family at the table.

Today, we carry her legacy forward. Our spices are ground by hand, our fish arrives fresh each morning, and our sweets are still set the traditional way in earthen pots. Every dish that leaves our kitchen honours the recipes she wrote in a tattered notebook, now our most treasured heirloom.

We don't just serve meals — we welcome you into a tradition of warm Bengali hospitality, or as we say, "Atithi Devo Bhava" — the guest is God.
