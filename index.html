Our Story

Every Recipe Carries a Grandmother's Bhalobasha

Ranna Ghor began in 1962 in a small tin-roofed kitchen in Bagbazar, where our great-grandmother Kamala-di ground mustard on a shil-nora every morning before sunrise. Her fish curry, seasoned only by instinct and love, became the reason neighbours lingered at the doorstep — and stayed for adda that ran long into the evening.

Three generations later, that same ranna ghor (kitchen) still guides everything we cook. The recipes have never been written down in exact measures — they live in memory, in the smell of mustard oil smoking in a kalai kada, and in the unhurried rhythm of a Sunday lunch table.

We still slow-cook in clay and iron, still hand-grind our spice pastes, and still believe a meal should feel like coming home to family — whoever you are, wherever you're from.

Hilsa Festival Special

For six weeks every monsoon, we bring in the finest Padma-fed Ilish and build an entire tasting thali around it — Shorshe Ilish, Bhapa Ilish, Ilish Bhaja and Doi Ilish, served with steamed gobindobhog rice.
